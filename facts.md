## Lebanon

- Lebanon was drawn into the conflict between Iran and Israel following the assassination of Iran's Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, by U.S. and Israeli forces on February 28, 2026. In response, Hezbollah, the Iran-backed Shia militia operating in Lebanon, launched a rocket and drone attack on a military site in Haifa, Israel, on March 2, 2026, marking its first direct strike on Israel since the November 2024 ceasefire.
- As of March 9, 2026, 486 people have been killed in Lebanon due to Israeli strikes, according to the Lebanese Health Ministry. More than 700,000 people have been displaced in Lebonon fromt he starting of the war.
- Lebanon is experiencing intense military escalation, with Israel launching a new wave of airstrikes across the country, particularly targeting Beirut's southern suburbs and southern Lebanon.
- Smoke rose over Beirut after an Israeli strike hit an apartment in the Ramada Hotel building in the Raouche district, killing at least four people and wounding ten others, according to Lebanon's health ministry.
- Israel has issued evacuation orders for residents in southern Lebanon, urging them to move north of the Litani River, causing massive traffic surges and displacement. The U.S., France, and the EU are urging restraint, with French President Emmanuel Macron calling on Israel not to expand operations and the EU's top diplomat warning of a potential "chaos" in Lebanon.
- Human Rights Watch (HRW) has found evidence Israel used white phosphorus in residential areas of southern Lebanon earlier this month, in violation of international humanitarian law.
